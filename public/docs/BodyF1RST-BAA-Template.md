## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides corporate wellness services that may involve the creation, receipt, maintenance, or transmission of Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of Covered Entity;

WHEREAS, Covered Entity and Business Associate desire to comply with the requirements of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), as amended by the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH);

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

Terms used in this Agreement have the same meaning as those terms in 45 C.F.R. Parts 160, 162, and 164.

**1.1 "Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** means individually identifiable health information as defined in 45 C.F.R. ยง 160.103.

**1.2 "Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means PHI transmitted or maintained in electronic media.

**1.3 "Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations.

**1.4 "Breach"** means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI as defined in 45 C.F.R. ยง 164.402.

---

## 2. PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES

**2.1 Service Performance.** Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only as necessary to perform the services specified in the Master Service Agreement.

**2.2 Management and Administration.** Business Associate may use PHI for its proper management and administration, and may disclose PHI for such purposes if:
- The disclosure is required by law; or
- Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that the PHI will be held confidentially

**2.3 Data Aggregation.** Business Associate may use PHI to provide Data Aggregation services relating to the health care operations of Covered Entity, provided such data is de-identified in accordance with 45 C.F.R. ยง 164.514.

**2.4 De-Identification.** Business Associate may de-identify PHI in accordance with 45 C.F.R. ยง 164.514.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**3.1 Limit Uses and Disclosures.** Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this Agreement or as required by law.

**3.2 Safeguards.** Business Associate shall implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI.

**3.3 Minimum Necessary.** Business Associate shall limit PHI use, disclosure, or request to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

**3.4 Reporting.** Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity:
- Any use or disclosure of PHI not provided for by this Agreement of which it becomes aware
- Any Security Incident of which it becomes aware
- Any Breach of Unsecured PHI

**3.5 Breach Notification Timeline.** Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity of any Breach of Unsecured PHI within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery.

**3.6 Subcontractors.** Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of Business Associate agree to the same restrictions and conditions as this Agreement.

**3.7 Access.** Business Associate shall make PHI available to Covered Entity as necessary for Covered Entity to respond to individual access requests.

**3.8 Amendment.** Business Associate shall incorporate any amendments to PHI as directed by Covered Entity.

**3.9 Accounting.** Business Associate shall maintain an accounting of disclosures and make such information available to Covered Entity.

**3.10 Compliance.** Business Associate shall comply with applicable requirements of the HIPAA Security Rule.

**3.11 HHS Access.** Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to PHI available to the Secretary of HHS for purposes of determining compliance.

---

## 4. SECURITY SAFEGUARDS

**4.1 Administrative Safeguards.** Business Associate implements:
- Designated Security Officer and Privacy Officer
- Workforce security training
- Access management policies
- Incident response procedures
- Risk assessments

**4.2 Physical Safeguards.** Business Associate implements:
- Facility access controls via SOC 2 certified data centers
- Workstation security policies
- Device and media controls

**4.3 Technical Safeguards.** Business Associate implements:
- AES-256 encryption for data at rest
- TLS 1.3 encryption for data in transit
- Unique user identification
- Automatic logoff
- Audit controls
- Integrity controls
- Multi-factor authentication

## 6. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

**6.1 Notices.** Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of:
- Any limitations in its Notice of Privacy Practices that affect Business Associate's use of PHI
- Any restrictions on use or disclosure agreed to with individuals
- Any changes to or revocation of authorization to use or disclose PHI

**6.2 Permissions.** Covered Entity shall not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate HIPAA.

---

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

**7.1 Term.** This Agreement is effective upon the Effective Date and continues until the Master Service Agreement terminates or this Agreement is terminated as provided herein.

**7.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement if the other party has materially breached and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of notice.

**7.3 Return or Destruction of PHI.** Upon termination, Business Associate shall:
- Return or destroy all PHI received from or created for Covered Entity
- Retain no copies of PHI except as necessary for legal or regulatory purposes
- If return or destruction is not feasible, extend protections of this Agreement

**7.4 Survival.** Obligations regarding PHI shall survive termination.

---

## 8. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**8.1 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only in writing signed by both parties. The parties shall amend this Agreement as necessary to comply with changes in HIPAA regulations.

**8.2 Interpretation.** This Agreement shall be interpreted in a manner consistent with HIPAA regulations.

**8.3 No Third-Party Beneficiaries.** Nothing in this Agreement creates rights in any third parties.

**8.4 Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by federal law (HIPAA) and, to the extent not preempted, the laws of the State of Texas.
